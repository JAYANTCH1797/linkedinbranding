# LinkedIn Hook Formulas Library

The hook is everything. LinkedIn shows only the first 1-2 lines before "see more".
You have ~7 words to earn the click. Use these formulas.

---

## CONTRARIAN HOOKS
- "Everyone talks about X. Nobody talks about Y."
- "Hot take: [conventional wisdom] is wrong."
- "Stop doing [common practice]. Here's what actually works."
- "The [industry] playbook is broken. Here's proof."
- "I used to believe [X]. Then [specific event] changed everything."

## CURIOSITY / OPEN LOOP
- "I made $X from [unexpected thing]. Here's how:"
- "The [metric] that nobody tracks but predicts everything."
- "3 years ago I failed at [X]. It was the best thing that happened."
- "This one decision [outcome]. The second-order effects were wild."
- "[Surprising result] in [timeframe]. What I learned:"

## NUMBER / LIST HOOKS
- "5 things [role] never tell you about [topic]:"
- "I've done [X]. Here are the [N] things that actually mattered:"
- "N signals your [product/team/strategy] is working (that aren't vanity metrics):"

## STORY HOOKS
- "6 months ago, [specific situation]. Today, [specific outcome]."
- "My manager told me [X]. I ignored it. Here's what happened:"
- "[Specific moment] taught me more about [topic] than [years of experience]."
- "We almost shut down [product/feature]. The reason we didn't:"

## DATA / INSIGHT HOOKS
- "[Surprising stat] and nobody's talking about why."
- "I analyzed [N] [items]. The pattern was clear:"
- "[Common belief] — but the data says otherwise."

## QUESTION HOOKS
- "Why do [smart people] keep making [mistake]?"
- "What if [assumption everyone makes] is completely wrong?"

---

## HOOK ANTI-PATTERNS (never use these)
- "I'm excited to share..." → boring, skip
- "Today I wanted to talk about..." → journalist-speak
- "As a [title], I often..." → ego-forward
- Starting with "I" as first word → weak
- Rhetorical questions with obvious answers → patronizing
